Optimized AI-based neural decoding from BOLD fMRI signal

Analyzing visual and semantic ROIs in the human visual system

Pasquale Anthony Della Rosa2, Paola Scifo2, Luca Mainardi1, Pietro Cerveri1,3
1Politecnico di Milano, 2Ospedale San Raffaele, 3Universitá di Pavia

Paper Code

Abstract

We introduce a novel two-stage AI architecture for reconstructing visual stimuli from fMRI BOLD signals. By leveraging a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) for non-linear mapping and a Latent Diffusion Model (LDM) guided by CLIP embeddings, we achieve competitive reconstruction fidelity while reducing first-stage complexity by 85%.

Our approach addresses the complexity and noisiness of fMRI data by splitting reconstruction into two phases: a coarse reconstruction using a VDVAE, followed by a refined generation using LDM. We further demonstrate that analyzing specific semantic ROIs (Floc) improves semantic coherence, while visual areas (V1-V4) maintain structural fidelity.

Methodology

The framework transforms fMRI BOLD signals into beta weights, maps them to a VAE latent space using a non-linear GRU network, and reconstructs the image using a Latent Diffusion Model conditioned on CLIP embeddings.
